XT Motherboard

XT full name is “Extended Technology“. XT motherboard was developed by IBM in 1983. In XT motherboard, use traditional Logic Board.

MINI ITX Motherboard

Mini-ITX motherboards are compact-sized boards designed for small form factor builds. They usually have a single PCIe slot, limited RAM slots, and are popular for compact gaming systems or HTPCs. Despite their size, they support capable CPUs and offer diverse connectivity options.

MICRO ATX Motherboard

Micro-ATX motherboards are a mid-sized form factor, larger than Mini-ITX but smaller than ATX. They typically offer more expansion slots than Mini-ITX, accommodating multiple PCIe cards, and are suitable for compact gaming or multimedia setups. Micro-ATX boards strike a balance between space efficiency and expandability in PC builds.

ATX Motherboard

ATX motherboards are standard-sized for desktop PCs, offering ample expansion slots, including multiple PCIe slots. Versatile and widely used, they support various components, making ATX a popular choice for gaming and general computing builds. Their standard form factor allows for compatibility with a wide range of cases and components.
